Ho Yeung Chim

Master Student studying in Uppsala University

Hi, I am Ho Yeung. I am currently a Master's student studying bioinformatics at Uppsala University in Sweden and working on Protein structure predictions in Arne Elofsson's lab in SciLife lab. My research interests are mainly in the application of deep learning in protein design and protein-protein interaction.

Prior to Uppsala, I received my bachelor's degree in Biochemistry and Cell Biology at Hong Kong University of Science and Technology in 2021. In HKUST, I used to work on synapse modeling with Prof. PARK, HyoKeun. I was also working on Medical Mycology with Prof. Patrick CY WOO in Hong Kong University Microbiology department.

In my spare time, I enjoy listening hip hop music and going on hikes with friends and family.

  1. Mycoses
    Rare/cryptic Aspergillus species infections and importance of antifungal susceptibility testing
    Tsang CC, Tang JYM, Ye H, Xing F, Lo SKF, Xiao C, Han L, Wu AKL, Ngan AHY, Law KC, To YN, Sze DTT, Chim OHY, Hui TWS, Leung CWH, Zhu T, Yao C, Tse BPH, Lau SKP, Woo PCY.
    Mycoses, Dec 2020

    The number of patients infected with Aspergillus rose dramatically in recent years. However, studies on the clinical spectrum and antifungal susceptibilities of non-classical (non-fumigatus, non-flavus, non-niger and non-terreus) pathogenic Aspergillus species are very limited. We examined the clinical spectrum and antifungal susceptibilities of 34 non-duplicated, non-classical Aspergillus isolates collected from Hong Kong, Shenzhen and Shanghai.
